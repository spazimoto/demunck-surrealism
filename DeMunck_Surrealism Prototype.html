What Is "Surrealism"?

With a goal defined as "[resolving] the previously contradictory conditions of dream and reality into an absolute reality, a super-reality, or surreality", surrealism is an art and cultural movement that uses the subconscious mind to express itself. Much of the art in this movement is largely non-sequitur and often unnerving or fantastical.


History

Originating in the early 20th century, surrealism was founded by French poet Andre Breton. The movement sought to "revolutionize the human experience", attempting to draw on the unconscious dream for inpsiration and exploration. This movement rose in the fall of the Dadaist movement, which was one of many art and cultural movements at the time that queried what exactly art was, and what was meant to be art. The surrealist movement pushed human creativity and thought, rivaling only cubism in it's ambitions.
